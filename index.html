Chromatin Architecture in Gene Regulation

PhD Research Project

Investigated the role of chromatin remodeling complexes in establishing and maintaining gene expression programs. Integrated ChIP-seq, ATAC-seq, and RNA-seq data to map regulatory landscapes and identify key chromatin modifiers involved in cellular differentiation.

Developed reproducible R-based pipelines for multi-omics data integration, revealing novel connections between chromatin accessibility and transcriptional output.

Built an automated analysis pipeline for processing and analyzing histone modification ChIP-seq data. The workflow includes quality control, peak calling, annotation, and differential binding analysis, with integrated visualization modules.

Implemented best practices for reproducibility including containerization, version control, and comprehensive documentation.

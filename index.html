About Me

I am a molecular biologist and recent PhD graduate specializing in chromatin biology and epigenetics. My research focuses on understanding how chromatin structure and modifications regulate gene expression and cellular function. I combine hands-on experimental expertise with computational approaches to generate reproducible, data-driven insights.

My approach emphasizes reproducible workflows, rigorous analysis, and the ability to communicate complex findings to diverse audiences—from technical specialists to broader scientific communities.

TET enzymes in RNA Regulation

PhD Research Project

Investigated the role of TET enzymes in establishing and maintaining gene expression programs. Integrated RNA-seq and hMeRIP-seq data to map regulatory landscapes and identify key regulators involved in oligodendrocyte lineage cells.

Developed reproducible R-based pipelines for multi-omics data integration, revealing novel connections between transcriptome and RNA modifications.

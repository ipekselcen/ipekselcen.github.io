Role of DNA Methylation in Oligodendrocyte Progenitor Cell Development

RNA-seq analysis investigating how Dnmt1-mediated DNA methylation regulates gene expression during early oligodendrocyte development in neonatal mice

Background

Oligodendrocyte progenitor cells (OPCs) are glial cells that differentiate into myelinating oligodendrocytes in the central nervous system. During postnatal development, OPCs undergo tightly regulated differentiation programs that require precise control of gene expression.

DNA methyltransferase 1 (Dnmt1) maintains DNA methylation patterns through cell division, serving as a critical epigenetic regulator. While DNA methylation is known to be essential for neural development, its specific role in OPC biology was poorly understood when this dataset was generated—making this one of the first studies to examine DNA methylation function in oligodendrocyte lineage cells.

This analysis investigates transcriptional changes in neonatal OPCs following Dnmt1 loss, revealing which genes and pathways depend on DNA methylation for proper regulation during early oligodendrocyte development.

Key Tool Choices & Rationale

STAR for Splice-Aware Alignment

STAR's two-pass alignment mode is essential for accurately mapping reads across splice junctions—critical when analyzing transcriptional changes that may affect splicing patterns. Particularly important for OPCs, which undergo dramatic transcriptional remodeling during differentiation.

DESeq2 for Robust Normalization

DESeq2's median-of-ratios normalization is more robust to outliers than simple RPKM/FPKM methods. Its empirical Bayes shrinkage of log fold changes is especially valuable with small sample sizes, improving accuracy of differential expression calls.

clusterProfiler for Comprehensive Enrichment

Enables both GO and KEGG pathway analysis in a unified framework with consistent statistical testing. Particularly useful for identifying coordinated changes in developmental pathways and epigenetic machinery.

Relevance to My Research

This analysis is directly relevant to my PhD work on epigenetic regulation in oligodendrocyte lineage cells. My dissertation focused on TET2-mediated RNA modifications in adult OPCs, examining how RNA hydroxymethylation (rather than DNA methylation) influences cell fate decisions.

Both Dnmt1 (DNA methyltransferase) and TET2 (which oxidizes methylcytosine) are epigenetic regulators critical for oligodendrocyte biology. Understanding how DNA methylation patterns established by Dnmt1 in neonatal OPCs compare to RNA modification patterns in adult OPCs provides insight into stage-specific epigenetic control mechanisms. The analytical approaches here mirror those I used in my published work in Cell Reports.

Reference Genome & Annotation

Genome: Mouse GRCm39 (mm39) from Ensembl release 115

Annotation: gencode.vM33.annotation.gtf

Why GRCm39: Latest mouse reference genome with improved assembly accuracy, particularly important for accurate mapping in repetitive genomic regions affected by DNA methylation.

Statistical Methods

Normalization: DESeq2 median-of-ratios

Dispersion estimation: Empirical Bayes shrinkage

Differential expression test: Wald test

Multiple testing correction: Benjamini-Hochberg FDR

Significance thresholds: FDR < 0.05, |log₂FC| > 1

Pathway enrichment: Hypergeometric test with FDR correction

Computational Requirements

CPUs: 16 cores recommended (8 minimum)

RAM: 64 GB for genome indexing, 32 GB for alignment

Storage: ~50 GB for raw data, ~30 GB for results

Runtime: ~4-6 hours on 16-core system

Code & Reproducibility

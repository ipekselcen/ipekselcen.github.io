H4K8 Acetylation Differentially Regulates Adult Oligodendrocyte Progenitors

ChIP-seq analysis revealing how an activating histone mark shapes the unique chromatin landscape and functional properties of adult brain progenitor cells

Dataset

GSE263808

Cell Type

PDGFRα+ OPCs

Comparison

Adult (P60) vs Neonatal (P5)

Samples

3 Adult, 3 Neonatal + Inputs

Publication: Dansu DK*, Selcen I*, et al. (2024). Histone H4 acetylation differentially modulates proliferation in adult oligodendrocyte progenitors. J Cell Biol 223(11):e202308064. [DOI]
*Co-first authors

Key Findings

  • 35,820 differential H4K8ac sites identified between adult and neonatal OPCs (FDR < 0.01)
  • 95% enriched in adult OPCs at functionally important genomic loci
  • Progenitor gene regulation with H4K8ac at Hes5, Gpr17, and Sox9
  • Metabolic gene priming at lipid metabolism genes (Txnip, Ptgds)
  • Myelin gene accessibility with H4K8ac at Cnp, Mog, and Mbp

Biological Context

Adult oligodendrocyte progenitor cells (aOPCs) represent a critical population of stem-like cells in the adult brain that maintain the capacity to generate new myelin-forming oligodendrocytes. Unlike their neonatal counterparts (nOPCs), aOPCs proliferate more slowly, respond differently to external signals, and express a unique transcriptional profile more similar to mature oligodendrocytes.

While transcriptional differences between aOPCs and nOPCs have been well-documented, the epigenetic mechanisms underlying these functional distinctions remained unclear. This study used unbiased histone proteomics to identify H4K8ac—an activating histone modification—as differentially enriched in adult progenitors.

Biological Interpretation

The Adult OPC Chromatin Landscape

Our ChIP-seq analysis reveals that adult OPCs maintain a unique chromatin state characterized by enhanced H4K8ac at three key functional categories:

  1. Progenitor identity genes — H4K8ac marks genes like Hes5, Sox9, and Gpr17, maintaining the stem-like progenitor state while preventing premature differentiation.
  2. Metabolic adaptation genes — Lipid metabolism genes (Txnip, Ptgds) show increased H4K8ac, reflecting the metabolic demands of adult CNS environment.
  3. Myelin gene priming — Despite being in the progenitor state, adult OPCs show H4K8ac at myelin genes (Cnp, Mog, Mbp), creating a "poised" chromatin state ready for rapid differentiation.

Functional Implications

The distinct H4K8ac landscape explains key functional differences between adult and neonatal OPCs:

  • Lower proliferation — Cell cycle genes lack H4K8ac in adult OPCs, consistent with their reduced proliferative capacity.
  • Metabolic specialization — Enhanced H4K8ac at metabolic genes supports the unique energy demands of adult progenitors.
  • Differentiation readiness — H4K8ac-marked myelin genes enable rapid response to demyelination or learning-induced plasticity.

Clinical Relevance

Understanding the epigenetic regulation of adult OPCs has important implications for myelin repair therapies in diseases such as multiple sclerosis, spinal cord injury, and age-related demyelination. The identification of H4K8ac as a key regulatory mark suggests potential therapeutic strategies targeting histone acetyltransferases to enhance remyelination capacity.

Methods Summary

Cell Isolation & ChIP: PDGFRα+ oligodendrocyte progenitors isolated from P5 (neonatal) and P60 (adult) Pdgfra-H2B-EGFP reporter mice via fluorescence-activated cell sorting. Chromatin immunoprecipitation performed with H4K8ac-specific antibody (Active Motif 61103) validated by western blot and peptide array.

Sequencing & Alignment: Libraries sequenced on Illumina HiSeq 4000 (paired-end, 150bp). Reads aligned to mm10 genome using Bowtie2 with --very-sensitive mode. BAM files filtered (MAPQ ≥ 20) and duplicates removed with Picard MarkDuplicates. Blacklist regions excluded.

Peak Calling: MACS2 used for broad peak calling (--broad --broad-cutoff 0.01) appropriate for histone modifications. Peaks called against matched input controls with minimum 200bp length.

Differential Binding: Differential H4K8ac occupancy analyzed using both MACS2 bdgdiff (fold change cutoff 3.0) and diffReps (window-based approach, FDR < 0.01). Sites identified by both methods considered high-confidence.

Annotation & Enrichment: ChIPseeker used for genomic feature annotation (TSS ± 3kb for promoters). Functional enrichment performed with GREAT and clusterProfiler using Gene Ontology and KEGG databases. Benjamini-Hochberg FDR correction applied.

Quality Control: All samples met quality thresholds: mapping rate >80%, duplication rate <25%, NSC >1.2, RSC >0.9, FRiP 3-8%.
